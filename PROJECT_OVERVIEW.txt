WHAT IS THIS PROJECT?
─────────────────────
AI-HPS is a smart digital assistant system built specifically for Hôpital
Général de Douala. It uses Artificial Intelligence to help three different
groups of people at the same time:

  1. PATIENTS AND VISITORS
     Instead of wandering the hospital trying to find the right department,
     a patient can open the app on their phone, type a question in plain
     English or French ("Where do I go for a blood test?" or "Où est la
     cardiologie?"), and receive immediate, accurate directions and
     guidance.

  2. DOCTORS AND NURSES (Clinical Staff)
     Medical staff can instantly look up the correct clinical procedure for
     any situation — for example, "What is the stroke protocol for a patient
     who arrived 20 minutes ago?" — and the AI provides step-by-step
     guidance sourced directly from World Health Organization (WHO)
     documents, tailored to HGD's own departments and resources.

  3. HOSPITAL ADMINISTRATORS
     Administrators get a dashboard where they can manage all procedures,
     approve protocol updates, monitor how staff are using the system,
     review audit logs, and track analytics — all in one secure web
     portal.

The system works on web browsers (for staff) and on mobile phones (for
patients), and was designed to work in Cameroon's bilingual context,
supporting both English and French.


────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
THE AI TECHNOLOGY — WHAT MAKES IT "INTELLIGENT"
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

This is not a simple search engine or a database lookup tool. The project
uses several advanced and modern AI technologies working together. Each is
explained below in plain terms.


1. LARGE LANGUAGE MODELS (LLMs) — The "Brain" of the System
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
A Large Language Model is a type of AI that can understand and generate
human language, much like a very knowledgeable expert you can have a
conversation with. The same technology powers ChatGPT.

In AI-HPS, three different LLMs are integrated:
  • GPT-4o-mini  (by OpenAI — the company behind ChatGPT)
  • Mistral      (a leading European AI model)
  • Gemini       (by Google DeepMind)

These models do not replace the doctor. They act as a highly informed
assistant — one that has read thousands of medical documents and can
instantly recall the right information. The system is designed so that
the AI always reminds users to verify clinical guidance with a senior
clinician before acting on it.


2. RAG — RETRIEVAL-AUGMENTED GENERATION
  "AI That Reads Documents Before It Answers"
───────────────────────────────────────────────
One of the biggest risks with AI is "hallucination" — when the AI
confidently gives a wrong answer that it invented. This is especially
dangerous in a medical setting.

AI-HPS uses a technique called Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG)
to solve this problem.

How it works, simply:
  Step 1 — Before the project went live, real WHO clinical documents
            (PDF guidebooks on Emergency Care, Maternity, ICU, Surgery,
            Blood Bank, and Infection Control) were fed into the system.

  Step 2 — When a doctor or nurse asks the AI a clinical question, the
            AI first SEARCHES those documents for the relevant section,
            then READS that section, and only THEN writes an answer
            based on what it actually found.

  Step 3 — If the AI's confidence score falls below 40%, it refuses to
            answer and instead tells the user it could not find reliable
            information. This is a deliberate safety feature — the
            system is designed to say "I don't know" rather than guess
            on a medical question.

This means every clinical answer the AI gives is traceable back to a
real WHO document stored in the system. It does not invent information.


3. VECTOR SEARCH (FAISS) — "Understanding Meaning, Not Just Words"
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Traditional hospital systems use keyword search — you type "stroke" and
it finds documents containing the word "stroke." This misses documents
that use the word "cerebrovascular accident" (the medical term for stroke)
or "brain attack" (a common alternative term).

AI-HPS uses FAISS (Facebook AI Similarity Search), a technology that
converts text into mathematical representations called "vectors" or
"embeddings." These capture the MEANING of words, not just the spelling.

In practice: if a nurse types "patient collapsed and is confused," the
system understands that this query is semantically related to stroke
protocols, sepsis guidelines, and hypoglycemia procedures — even without
those exact words being typed. It retrieves all relevant content by
understanding the intent behind the words.

This makes the search dramatically smarter than any keyword-based system.


4. MULTI-AGENT AI PIPELINE (LangGraph)
  "A Team of Specialist AIs Working Together"
──────────────────────────────────────────────
Most AI systems use a single AI model to handle every type of question.
AI-HPS takes a more advanced approach: it uses a pipeline of six
SPECIALISED AI AGENTS, each designed for a specific type of task. They
are coordinated using a framework called LangGraph, which is a
state-of-the-art tool for building AI agent networks.

Think of it as a hospital itself — when you arrive, a receptionist
(Router Agent) figures out what you need and directs you to the right
specialist. You do not see the cardiologist for a broken arm.

The six agents are:

  │          │  navigation question? A clinical procedure lookup?    │

The pipeline always follows this pattern:

  AGENT-R  →  (Emergency?) → AGENT-E → AGENT-O → Answer
           →  (Navigation?) → AGENT-N → AGENT-O → Answer
           →  (Information?) → AGENT-C → AGENT-O → Answer
           →  (Procedure?) → [AGENT-C →] AGENT-P → AGENT-O → Answer


5. NATURAL LANGUAGE PROCESSING (NLP) — "Understanding Human Language"
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The system uses NLP techniques to:

  • LANGUAGE DETECTION: Automatically detect whether the user is
    writing in English or French, and respond in the same language.
    This is critical for Cameroon's bilingual healthcare context.

  • INTENT CLASSIFICATION: Understand WHAT the user is trying to do,
    not just what words they typed. A question like "my arm hurts after
    the surgery" is correctly identified as a post-operative concern,
    not a navigation or general information request.

  • SEMANTIC SIMILARITY: Match what a user says to the closest relevant
    department, procedure, or content in the knowledge base, even when
    the exact words do not match.


6. KNOWLEDGE BASE INGESTION — "Teaching the AI from Real Documents"
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The AI's clinical knowledge does not come from the general internet
(which contains unreliable medical content). It was specifically trained
on the following WHO and international clinical guideline documents:

  • ICU:              WHO Critical Care guidelines (2 volumes)
  • Blood Bank:       WHO Clinical Use of Blood, Patient Blood Management
  • Maternity:        WHO Safe Childbirth, Antenatal Care, Labour guides
  • Surgery:          WHO Safe Surgery and Paediatric Surgery manuals
  • Infection Control: WHO Infection Prevention and Control documents

A custom data ingestion pipeline extracts text from these PDF documents,
processes them into the vector database, and makes them searchable by the
AI. This process is called "knowledge base ingestion."

The system is designed so that as HGD's own internal protocols are
approved and added by administrators, those become part of the knowledge
base too — continuously improving the AI's knowledge.


────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
HOW IS THIS PROJECT INNOVATIVE?
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

1. BUILT FOR AN AFRICAN HOSPITAL CONTEXT
   Most hospital AI systems are built for hospitals in Europe or the USA
   with high-bandwidth internet, expensive devices, and staff who speak
   one language. AI-HPS was designed for HGD Douala, accounting for:
   • Bilingual users (English and French)
   • Patients who may only have a basic feature phone (supported via USSD
     — the same technology used for mobile money in Africa)
   • A resource-constrained environment where the AI must be fast, safe,
     and work within the hospital's own server infrastructure.

2. SAFETY-FIRST AI DESIGN
   The system has a hard confidence threshold: if the AI is not at least
   40% confident in its clinical answer, it refuses to answer. Most
   commercial chatbots do not have this feature — they always produce an
   answer even when they are not sure. In a medical environment, a wrong
   confident answer can be fatal. The AI in this system is designed to
   say "I am not certain" when appropriate.

3. TAMPER-PROOF AUDIT SYSTEM
   Every single action in the system — every login, every procedure
   viewed, every AI query — is recorded in an append-only audit log that
   is cryptographically signed using HMAC (Hash-Based Message
   Authentication Code). This means it is mathematically impossible to
   alter or delete a log entry without the system detecting it.
   This is a feature typically found in banking and legal systems, and
   is being applied here to healthcare for accountability and
   patient safety.

4. MULTI-PLATFORM FROM ONE AI ENGINE
   The same AI pipeline produces correctly formatted responses for five
   different output channels: web browser, mobile app, SMS, WhatsApp,
   and USSD. No other known hospital information system in Cameroon
   provides AI guidance across all these channels from a single engine.

5. MULTI-AGENT ARCHITECTURE
   Rather than a single AI trying to be good at everything (and
   consequently good at nothing), AI-HPS uses a network of six
   specialized agents — the same architectural approach used by
   leading AI research organizations for complex real-world systems.
   This architecture makes the system more accurate, faster, safer,
   and easier to maintain and upgrade.

6. REAL-TIME ANALYTICS AND CONTENT GAP DETECTION
   The system tracks which questions the AI COULD NOT answer with
   confidence. These are stored as "content gaps" and reported to
   administrators, telling them exactly which clinical areas need more
   documentation added to the system. The AI effectively tells the
   hospital where its knowledge is incomplete.

AI-HPS is an intelligent, bilingual hospital assistant that uses a team of
six specialised AI agents — backed by WHO clinical documents and the same
language model technology that powers ChatGPT — to guide patients through

hospital protocols, all through a single secure system built specifically for
the Cameroonian healthcare context.
